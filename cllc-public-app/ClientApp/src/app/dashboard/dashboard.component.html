A non-medical cannabis retail store licence allows licensees to sell non-medical cannabis and cannabis accessories in British Columbia.

To apply for a non-medical cannabis retail store licence, applicants must submit a completed application form and completed business financial integrity form. A separate application and business financial integrity form is required for each proposed establishment.

The Liquor and Cannabis Regulation Branch (LCRB) will only process complete applications. The checklist accompanying the application form can assist in completing all required documentation.

Please note that applications which are submitted without all required information or documents will not be processed. Applicants will be contacted and provided the opportunity to submit missing information. Once applicants provide all required documents to the LCRB, the application will be submitted for processing.

Inquires related to your application can be directed to cannabis.licensing@gov.bc.ca.

ASSOCIATES

SECURITY SCREENING

An applicant that is a Private Corporation may have associates that are required to consent to security screening and financial integrity assessments as part of the application for a non-medical cannabis retail store licence. Applicants are required to provide associates with the Parcel Identifier (PID) or proposed retail store location address.

Parcel Identifier (PID):
  • The PID is a nine-digit number permanently assigned to a parcel of land.
  • The legal description and PID may be found on a Land Title Certificate, in BC Property Assessment documents, on the lease, or obtained from the landlord.

Applicants should direct associates to the LCRB non-medical cannabis webpage to download required forms and instructions for submitting documentation.

Associates will submit documentation for security screening and financial integrity assessments directly to the LCRB. Applicants should not delay submitting a completed application form and business financial integrity form. It is the applicant's responsibility to direct all associates to the link provided.

Associates of Private Corporation include:
  • Directors and officers
  • Generally, shareholders holding at least 10% or more of voting shares (the Liquor and Cannabis Regulation Branch has the discretion to screen shareholders holding less than 10%)

Corporations, societies and/or partnerships that are shareholders in the Private Corporation are considered associates of the Applicant and are required to submit an associate organization assessment form.
