ASSOCIATES

SECURITY SCREENING

An applicant that is a Private Corporation may have associates that are required to submit additional documentation as part of the application process.

Associates of a Private Corporation may include:
  • Directors and officers
  • Generally, shareholders holding at least 10% or more of voting shares (the Liquor and Cannabis Regulation Branch has the discretion to require shareholders holding less than 10% to submit additional documentation)
  • Corporations, societies and/or partnerships that are shareholders in the Private Corporation are considered associates of the Applicant and are required to submit an associate organization assessment form. The LCRB may identify additional associates during processing of the application, and those associates may be required to submit additional documentation as part of the application process.
Once the application is submitted, provide associates with:
  • The name of the applicant
  • The application number
  • The address of the proposed cannabis retail store (if applicable)
  • A link to the Associate Forms page, which contains the instructions and required forms for associates.

An applicant that is a Public Corporation may have associates that are required to submit additional documentation as part of the application process.

Associates of a Public Corporation include:
  • Directors and officers
Once the application is submitted, provide associates with:
  • The name of the applicant
  • The application number
  • The address of the proposed cannabis retail store (if applicable)
  • A link to the Associate Forms page, which contains the instructions and required forms for associates.

The LCRB may identify additional associatesduring processing of the application, and those associates may be required to submit additional documentation as part of the application process.

An applicant that is a Partnership may have associates that are required to submit additional documentation as part of the application process.

Associates of a Partnership include:
  • All partners
  • If a limited partnership, limited partners holding at least a 10% interest in the partnership (the Liquor and Cannabis Regulation Branch has the discretion to screen partners holding less than 10%)

Corporations, societies and/or partnerships that are partners in the Partnership are considered associates of the Applicant and are required to submit an associate organization assessment form.

Once the application is submitted, provide associates with:
  • The name of the applicant
  • The application number
  • The address of the proposed cannabis retail store (if applicable)
  • A link to the Associate Forms page, which contains the instructions and required forms for associates.

An applicant that is a Society may have associates that are required to submit additional documentation as part of the application process.

Associates of a Society include:
  • Directors and officers
  • Senior Managers
Once the application is submitted, provide associates with:
  • The name of the applicant
  • The application number
  • The address of the proposed cannabis retail store (if applicable)
  • A link to the Associate Forms page, which contains the instructions and required forms for associates.

The LCRB be identify additional associates during processing of the application, and those associates may be required to submit additional documentation as part of the application process.

An applicant that is a Sole Proprietorship must consent to a security screening as part of the application for a licence.

Please visit the LCRB non-medical cannabis webpage to download required forms and instructions for submitting documentation for the security screening directly to the LCRB.

ASSOCIATES FOR INDIGENOUS NATIONS

An applicant that is an Indigenous Nation may have associates that are required to consent to security screening and financial integrity assessments as part of the application for a non-medical cannabis retail store licence. Applicants are required to provide associates with the Parcel Identifier (PID) or proposed retail store location address.

Parcel Identifier (PID):
  • The PID is a nine-digit number permanently assigned to a parcel of land.
  • The legal description and PID may be found on a Land Title Certificate, in BC Property Assessment documents, on the lease, or obtained from the landlord.

Applicants should direct associates to the LCRB non-medical cannabis webpage to download required forms and instructions for submitting documentation.

Associates will submit documentation for security screening and financial integrity assessments directly to the LCRB. Applicants should not delay submitting a completed application form and business financial integrity form. It is the applicant's responsibility to direct all associates to the link provided.

Associates for Indigenous Nations applications include all persons who are authorized to act on behalf of the nation.

If you require more information please email Cannabis.Licensing@gov.bc.ca.
