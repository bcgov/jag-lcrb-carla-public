Application Checklist

Make sure you have this information on hand before you start your Special Event Permit application.

Event Information:
  • Location, date, time & guest count
Applying on behalf of a Business or Organization:
  • You need a business number issued by Canada Revenue Agency (CRA)
Events on public property:
  • Events  held on public property must have written approval from local government or First Nations
    • For more information, review Section 10 of the  Special Event Permit Policy Manual (PDF, 533KB)
Serving It Right (SIR) or Special Event Server (SES) certification:
  • If you have already completed training, enter your certificate number when applying
  • You can apply for your liquor Special Event Permit now and complete training later
    • You and anyone who may serve liquor at your event must complete a training program before your event starts
  • Special Event Server is required for all servers and for small events with fewer than 500 attendees
  • Serving it Right course is required for permit holders and event managers of large events with more than 500 attendees
  • Learn more about  certifications
Drinks:
  • What types of drinks will you serve and how many
  • Your proposed drink pricing
    • Maximum drink prices are set by the Liquor and Cannabis Regulation Branch (LCRB) and outlined in Appendix 2 of the   Special Event Permit Policy Manual (PDF, 533KB)
    • You may be able to apply for an exemption to  charge more than the maximum drink price at your event
Fees and Payment:

Fees:
  • Public special events, $100.00 per day/location
  • Private special events $25.00 per day/location
Payment:
  • Payment methods:
    • Visa, MasterCard, American Express, Visa Debit
  • Payment process:
    1. Submit application
    2. Once application is approved, you will receive a notification by email prompting you to pay
    3. Log in and pay for your Special Event Permit
    4. Permit is issued

For more information about holding special events, review the  Special Event Permit Terms and Conditions Handbook (PDF, 433KB).
