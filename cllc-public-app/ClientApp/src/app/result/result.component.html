What the applicant needs to prepare

Age Eligibility

  • An applicant is not eligible to complete an application for a cannabis retail store licence if they are under 19 years old. No one can operate a cannabis retail licensed business who is under 19.

Federal licensed producer of cannabis

The applicant is not eligible for a cannabis retail store licence if they are the owner of a business which is a federally licensed producer of cannabis.

Applying as an Indigenous nation in its own name, rather than as a sole proprietor, partnership or corporation

Applicants applying as an Indigenous nation must submit the:

  • Full name of Indigenous nation applying for the licence
  • Contact information (mailing address, email, phone number)
  • Business Number (nine-digit number issued by the Canada Revenue Agency)
  • Representatives by:
    • Full legal name
    • Business contact information

The authorized person in the Indigenous nation will go through a security screen and a financial integrity check during the application process.

Please note: Indigenous nations applying in their own name rather than as a sole proprietor, partnership, corporation or society do not apply through the portal but have their own process. Contact the LCRB at Cannabis.Licensing@gov.bc.ca for more information.

Registered business

Applicants, other than Indigenous nations, will need to be registered in B.C. as sole proprietors, partnerships or corporations. Indigenous nation applicants may apply in their own name or as one of the legal entities above. The type of business that an applicant operates will determine who involved with the business will need to undergo security screening, a financial integrity check and what additional information the applicant needs to submit. The applicant can register a sole proprietor or partnership at OneStop Business Registry or a corporation at Corporate Online. The business should also register for a Business BCeID.

Information about the store

The store must be in a self-contained location. Licensees must not operate a cannabis retail store as part of another business, such as a pharmacy, restaurant or a liquor store.

The store's property arrangement must be valid for at least 12 months after the licence is issued.

During the application, the applicant will need to submit the following pieces of information about the store's location:

  • PID (either a nine-digit number or a legal description of the property)
  • Photos/renderings of the store’s exterior and signage
  • A floor plan showing:
    • All doors leading into/out of the sales area
    • All entrances to the store (including back doors)
    • The locations of all fixed shelving and/or display cases
    • The locations of all cash registers
    • Which areas are public and which are for staff only
    • The locations of all storage areas
  • A site plan showing:
    • The location of the building and where it’s located on the property
    • The location of all entrances to the store
    • The locations of other buildings and businesses nearby, the entrances to those buildings, and their names or a description of what they are (e.g. gas station, office building)
    • Relevant street names nearby
    • Road access to the property, including the locations of any driveways and parking spots

Connections to Federally Licensed Cannabis Producers

Restrictions will be placed on a retail store that has a close business, financial or family connection to a producer. Licensees will not be permitted to enter agreements to sell a producer’s product in preference to others. A licence will not be issued if the Liquor and Cannabis Regulation Branch (LCRB) is of the opinion that the association between the licensee and producer is so close that the licensee is likely to promote the sale of cannabis of the federally licensed producer. In making this determination, the LCRB will consider whether:

  • The licensee holds or has a beneficial interest in, directly or indirectly, 20% or more of the producer's voting shares
  • An immediate family member has any financial interest in the producer
  • The producer holds or has a beneficial interest in, directly or indirectly, 20% or more of the cannabis retail business's voting shares
  • Any person, partnership or company holds or has a beneficial interest in, directly or indirectly, 20% or more of both the cannabis retail store's and the producer's voting shares

When making a licence application, applicants must declare if they or any business partners or an immediate family member of the applicant or any business partner has any ownership in a federally licensed cannabis producer, even if less than 20%.

Application Fee

Applicants must pay a $7,500 application fee to apply for a licence. The application fee includes the cost of the initial required security screening and financial integrity checks.

If approved for a licence, licensees must pay a licensing fee of $1,500.
