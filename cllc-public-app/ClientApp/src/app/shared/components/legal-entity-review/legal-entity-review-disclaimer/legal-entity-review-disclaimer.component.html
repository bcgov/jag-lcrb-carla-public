About the Application

The Liquor and Cannabis Regulation Branch (the Branch) has determined a review of your legal entity is necessary to ensure you are complying with reporting requirements. Information about reporting requirements can be found in the Terms and ConditionsHandbooks.

Per the authority under Section 59(2) of the Liquor Control and Licensing Act, you received the below notice by email which contains more information about the process.

You must update your tied house declarations and/or upload corporate documents for your organization to complete the Legal Entity Review. Please follow the instructions in your notice letter.

Generally, tied house relationships between licensees (specifically liquor primary, food primary, private liquor/wine store, or the business location of a caterer) and manufacturers or agents are prohibited under the Act. "Tied house" refers to a relationship between a manufacturer/agent and another licensee if the relationship puts the licensee in the position of being likely to promote the sale of that manufacturer's/agent's liquor products over another manufacturer. A tied house relationship may result in the licensee benefitting more when they sell the liquor of that manufacturer's/agent's liquor.

Please complete this Legal Entity Review as soon as possible; you cannot apply for a Permanent Change to Licensee application, or submit Transfer of Licence or Third Party Operator applications while the Legal Entity Review application is unresolved.

If you have any questions about this application, contact the Branch at LCRBLiquor@gov.bc.ca
